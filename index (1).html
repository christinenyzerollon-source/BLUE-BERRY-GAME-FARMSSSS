History:
Blue Berry Game Farm was started in 2003 by Hilario Tanato,who began raising gamefowl as a personal hobby. His dedication to breeding healthy and strong birds quickly caught the attention of people in the community. As more customers trusted his gamefowl, Hilario officially named the farm “Blue Berry Game Farm.” Through hard work and careful breeding, the farm grew and became known for producing quality gamefowl that many buyers rely on today.

Feature The Owner

Hilario Tanato lives a life rooted in hard work, instinct, and deep passion as the driving force behind Blue Berry Game Farm. Known for his quiet dedication, Hilario starts each day before daylight, walking through the farm with a practiced eye—checking broods, studying bloodlines, and observing the health and behavior of every bird under his care. What began as a small backyard setup gradually grew into a respected game farm because of his patience, discipline, and commitment to proper breeding standards. He carefully selects his breeders based on performance, temperament, and body structure, believing that every champion starts with a well-chosen pair. More than a breeder, Hilario is a hands-on worker and a mentor to his team, teaching them proper conditioning, responsible gamefowl handling, and the importance of consistency. His life revolves around the rhythm of the farm—sunrise routines, feeding schedules, conditioning work, and continuous improvement. For Hilario, Blue Berry Game Farm is not just a business but a lifelong passion and legacy built on perseverance, humility, and love for the craft.

The Success story of Blue Berry Game Farm

The success of a game farm business relies on maintaining strong bloodlines, proper breeding management, and providing excellent care for every bird. Quality feeding, clean facilities, and consistent health programs help ensure strong performance and high survival rates. Success also depends on discipline in selection, keeping only the best breeders to preserve traits like power, intelligence, and durability. Good record-keeping, reliable partnerships, and a solid reputation further strengthen the farm’s growth. When these factors work together, the game farm becomes productive, sustainable, and well-respected in the community.

Challenges Faced By the Founder/s

The founder faced many challenges in building the game farm, starting with limited resources and the struggle to acquire quality breeding materials. He had to learn through experience, often dealing with setbacks such as disease outbreaks, harsh weather, and losses that tested his patience and determination. Managing the farm alone in the early years required long hours, physical work, and constant problem-solving. He also needed to earn the trust of buyers and fellow breeders, which took time and consistent results. Despite these difficulties, the founder’s passion, discipline, and commitment helped him overcome each challenge and shape the farm into a stronger, more successful business.

The business will be seen after 10 years:

After 10 years, the game farm will be seen as a stable, trusted, and well-established business known for quality and consistency. It will have strong bloodlines, improved facilities, and a wider network of loyal customers and fellow breeders. Through years of experience, careful breeding, and proper management, the farm will continue to produce high-quality stock and maintain a solid reputation. With sustained growth and adaptation to new practices, the business will stand as a long-term success and a model of dedication and excellence in the game farm industry.

MESSAGE FROM THE OWNER

How did the government of the Philippines help your business grow?

The government of the Philippines helped the business grow by providing guidelines, regulations, and support programs that promoted responsible farming and entrepreneurship. Through local government units, the business gained access to permits, agricultural advice, and training that improved farm management and biosecurity practices. Government-supported seminars and extension services helped increase knowledge in breeding, animal health, and farm sustainability. In addition, infrastructure development and access to agricultural programs created a more stable environment for operations and market growth. These forms of support contributed to the steady development and legitimacy of the game farm business.
